Appendix A Technical & QA

Cold Chain Operations (Dates)

Recommended Claim Language

Use this phrasing in public channels until third-party verification is published:

  • Preferred: “Advanced cold chain operations for dates across storage and last-mile delivery.”
  • Only if verified: “Industry-first cold chain for dates on quick commerce.” (requires third-party citation)

Technical Specifications (Summary)

  • Temperature band: 5–10°C (target 7°C)
  • Product applicability: Dates (all varieties and sizes)
  • Process coverage: Inbound → cold storage → pick/pack → quick commerce handoff → insulated last mile
  • Validation: Time–temperature data loggers; weekly calibration; exception alerting

Operational SOP (Abbreviated)

  1. Inbound QC: COA check; random sample inspection; lot code registration
  2. Cold Storage: Continuous logging at 5–10°C; alarm at 10°C+
  3. Pick/Pack: Max 15 minutes out-of-cold; insulated staging bins
  4. Quick Commerce Handoff: Insulated totes; rider SLA ≤ 30 minutes
  5. Audit: Weekly logger review; monthly SOP compliance audit

Evidence Plan

  • Publish redacted logger screenshots (date/lot) monthly on website
  • Third-party facility audit summary (FSSC/BRC scope notes)
  • Photographic evidence: cold rooms, insulated totes, probes in use

Based on the competitive landscape research:

  • Preferred (Conservative): "Advanced cold chain operations for dates across storage and last-mile delivery."

Appendix B Quality & CX

Contamination QC & Customer Response Playbook

Zero-Tolerance Policy

Any report of contamination (worms/insects/foreign matter) triggers immediate replacement/refund and full root-cause analysis.

Customer Triage (T+0)

  1. Acknowledge within 2 business hours; apologize; request order id, photos, and lot code
  2. Offer immediate replacement or refund; arrange pickup for sample if feasible

Internal Protocol (T+1)

  1. Quarantine affected lot; increase inspection frequency
  2. Review COA, storage logs, and handling records for the lot
  3. Conduct supplier review and corrective action if upstream indicators exist

Root Cause & CAPA (T+3 to T+7)

  1. Document investigation (5-Why/Fishbone)
  2. Implement corrective actions (storage, sealing, supplier sorting)
  3. Publish brief summary internally; update training materials

Public Communication Templates

Short Form: “We’re sorry—this shouldn’t happen. We’ve replaced/refunded your order. We’ve initiated a quality review of this batch and tightened checks. Thank you for flagging this so we can make it right.”

Follow-up (if systemic): “Following your report, we quarantined the batch, reviewed cold logs and sealing checks, and updated our SOPs. We’ve also reinforced supplier screening. Your trust matters; thank you for helping us improve.”
